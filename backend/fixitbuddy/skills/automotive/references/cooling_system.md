## Diagnostic Steps

**IMPORTANT: Always check cooling system with engine COLD (off for 2+ hours)**

1. **Locate the coolant reservoir** — translucent plastic container with MIN/MAX marks
   - *Camera cue: Usually labeled "COOLANT" or has a radiator/temperature symbol. Fluid is green, orange, pink, or blue depending on type*

2. **Check coolant level** against MIN/MAX marks
   - *Camera cue: Level should be between MIN and MAX when cold*

3. **Check coolant color and condition**
   - Bright color (green/orange/pink) = good
   - Brown/rusty = contaminated, needs flush
   - Oily = engine oil mixing in = serious issue

4. **Look under the car for puddles**
   - *Camera cue: Bright-colored sweet-smelling liquid = coolant leak*

5. **Check radiator hoses** — squeeze them (engine cold), should feel firm but pliable
   - *Camera cue: Cracked, soft, or mushy hoses = replace soon*

6. **Check radiator cap condition** (engine COLD ONLY)
   - *Camera cue: Cracked or worn rubber seal = can't hold pressure = overheating*

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Temperature gauge in the red / warning light | Low coolant, stuck thermostat, or failed water pump | **STOP driving immediately.** Let engine cool 30+ min. Add coolant when cold |
| Steam from under hood | Coolant leak hitting hot engine | **STOP immediately.** Do not open hood until steam stops. Check coolant when cold |
| Heater blowing cold air | Low coolant level or stuck thermostat (open) | Check coolant level. If full, thermostat likely stuck open |
| Coolant level keeps dropping | Leak in hoses, radiator, water pump, or head gasket | Inspect hoses for wet spots/cracks. Pressure test needed if no visible leak |
| Sweet smell from engine bay | Coolant leak | Find source — check hose connections, radiator, and water pump weep hole |
| White smoke from exhaust | Coolant burning in combustion chamber | Likely head gasket failure. Do not drive. Professional diagnosis needed |

## Coolant Types — Do NOT Mix
- Green (traditional) — ethylene glycol
- Orange/red (OAT) — organic acid technology (GM DexCool)
- Blue/pink (HOAT) — hybrid organic (many European/Asian cars)
- Check your owner's manual for the correct type

## Safety Notes
- **NEVER open the radiator cap on a hot engine** — pressurized steam causes severe burns
- Let engine cool **at least 30 minutes** before touching cooling system components
- Coolant is **toxic to pets** — sweet smell attracts animals. Clean up all spills immediately
- If overheating occurs while driving: turn heater to MAX (draws heat from engine), pull over safely, turn off engine. Do not idle
