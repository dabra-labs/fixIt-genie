# Engine Oil System

**Category:** Automotive
**Covers:** Engine lubrication, oil level, oil pressure, oil condition

## Error Codes
| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| P0520 | Oil Pressure Sensor/Switch Circuit Malfunction |
| P0521 | Oil Pressure Sensor/Switch Range/Performance |
| P0522 | Oil Pressure Sensor/Switch Low Input |
| P0523 | Oil Pressure Sensor/Switch High Input |
| P0524 | Oil Pressure Too Low |

## Diagnostic Steps

1. **Locate the oil dipstick** — usually has a yellow or orange handle
   - *Camera cue: Look for a looped handle near the engine block*

2. **Pull the dipstick out and wipe clean** with a rag or paper towel
   - *Camera cue: The dipstick has two marks — MIN and MAX*

3. **Reinsert fully, then pull out again** to read the level
   - *Camera cue: Oil should be between MIN and MAX marks. Below MIN = add oil immediately*

4. **Check oil color and condition**
   - Amber/honey = good
   - Dark brown = due for change but OK
   - Black = overdue for change
   - Milky/frothy = coolant mixing with oil (serious — head gasket issue)
   - *Camera cue: Wipe dipstick on white cloth to see color clearly*

5. **Add oil if needed** through the oil filler cap on top of the engine
   - *Camera cue: Cap often has an oil can icon or says "OIL"*
   - Add oil in small amounts (0.5 qt), recheck level each time

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Ticking/tapping noise from engine | Low oil level — insufficient lubrication | Top up to MAX mark. If noise persists after adding oil, check for leak underneath |
| Oil pressure warning light on | Low oil pressure — low level, worn pump, or sensor issue | Check level first. If level OK, DO NOT DRIVE — have towed to mechanic |
| Oil looks milky/frothy | Coolant mixing with oil — possible head gasket failure | Do not drive. Needs professional diagnosis immediately |
| Engine burning oil (blue smoke from exhaust) | Worn piston rings or valve seals | Monitor level closely. Add oil as needed. Plan for engine repair |
| Oil leak under car | Gasket failure, drain plug, or oil filter | Identify source. Small leak: monitor level. Large leak: repair before driving |

## Safety Notes
- Engine may be hot — let cool **10+ minutes** before checking oil
- Keep hands away from belts and cooling fans (even when engine is off — can spin unexpectedly)
- Oil is slippery — clean any spills immediately to prevent falls
- Used oil is hazardous waste — dispose at auto parts store, not in trash
