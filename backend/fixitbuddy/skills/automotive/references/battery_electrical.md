# Car Battery and Electrical System

**Category:** Automotive
**Covers:** Battery, alternator, starting system, electrical diagnostics

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| P0562 | System Voltage Low |
| P0563 | System Voltage High |

| P0621 | Generator Lamp Control Circuit Malfunction |

## Diagnostic Steps

1. **Locate the battery** — usually in the engine bay, sometimes in the trunk or under the rear seat
   - *Camera cue: Rectangular box with two terminals — red (+) and black (−)*

2. **Check for corrosion on terminals**
   - *Camera cue: White, blue-green, or grey crusty buildup on terminals = corrosion*

3. **Check if battery terminals are tight** — they shouldn't wiggle
   - *Camera cue: Loose connection is a common cause of intermittent starting issues*

4. **Clean corroded terminals** with baking soda + water and a wire brush or terminal cleaning tool
   - *Camera cue: Terminals should be shiny metal after cleaning*

5. **Check battery age** — most batteries last 3-5 years
   - *Camera cue: Date sticker on battery top (format: month/year or quarter/year)*

## Jump Starting Procedure
1. Connect red (+) to dead battery positive
2. Connect red (+) to good battery positive
3. Connect black (−) to good battery negative
4. Connect black (−) to an unpainted metal surface on the dead car (NOT the battery negative)
5. Start the working car, wait 2-3 minutes, then try starting the dead car
6. Remove cables in REVERSE order

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Clicking sound when turning key | Weak battery or poor connection | Clean terminals. Try jump start. If clicks once = bad starter solenoid |
| No sound at all when turning key | Completely dead battery or blown fuse | Jump start or replace battery. Check main fuse if jump doesn't work |
| Car starts then dies | Alternator not charging battery | Check alternator belt. Have alternator tested (free at most auto parts stores) |
| Battery keeps dying | Parasitic drain, bad alternator, or old battery | Leave car off 30 min with nothing running. Voltage should be 12.4V+ |
| Dashboard warning lights flicker | Loose battery connection or failing alternator | Check terminal tightness. Check alternator belt tension |

## Voltage Reference
- Battery at rest (engine off): 12.4–12.7V = good, 12.0V = weak, below 12V = needs charge/replacement
- Engine running (alternator charging): 13.8–14.5V = good, below 13V = alternator issue

## Safety Notes
- Battery acid is corrosive — **wear gloves and eye protection**
- Never short the terminals together — risk of explosion and severe burns
- Remove **negative (−) terminal first** when disconnecting, reconnect **last**
- If battery is cracked, bulging, or leaking — do not attempt to charge, replace immediately
- Hydrogen gas accumulates around batteries — keep sparks and flames away
