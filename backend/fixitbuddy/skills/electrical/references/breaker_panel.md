# Residential Breaker Panel

**Category:** Electrical
**Covers:** Circuit breakers, tripped breakers, power outages, panel diagnostics

## Diagnostic Steps

1. **Open the breaker panel door**
   - *Camera cue: Metal door, usually in garage, basement, utility closet, or hallway*

2. **Look for any breaker in the middle position** (not fully ON or OFF)
   - *Camera cue: A tripped breaker sits between ON and OFF — it won't be aligned with the others. Often has a red or orange indicator window*

3. **To reset a tripped breaker:**
   a. Push the tripped breaker fully to **OFF** first
   b. Then flip firmly to **ON**
   - *Camera cue: You should hear/feel a click when it engages in ON position*

4. **If breaker trips again immediately** — there is a fault on that circuit
   - Do NOT keep resetting
   - Unplug everything on that circuit, then reset
   - Add devices back one at a time to find the culprit

5. **If breaker won't reset at all** — the breaker itself may be failed
   - This requires a licensed electrician

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| One room lost power | Tripped breaker — often overloaded circuit | Find and reset the tripped breaker. If it trips again, reduce load on that circuit |
| Breaker keeps tripping | Overloaded circuit, short circuit, or ground fault | Unplug everything on circuit, reset, add devices back one at a time |
| Half the house lost power | Two-pole breaker tripped, or utility power issue | Check for double-width breaker in panel. Call utility if all breakers look OK |
| No power at all | Main breaker tripped or utility outage | Check main breaker at top of panel. Call utility company to confirm outage |
| GFCI outlet won't reset | Ground fault on circuit, or failed GFCI | Unplug everything from GFCI outlets on that circuit. Press RESET. If still won't reset, the GFCI needs replacement |
| Burning smell from panel | Loose connection, overheated wire, or failing breaker | **DO NOT TOUCH. Leave the area. Call an electrician immediately.** |

## Breaker Size Reference
- 15A circuits: general lighting, most bedroom outlets
- 20A circuits: kitchen outlets, bathroom outlets, garage outlets
- 30A circuits: dryers, water heaters, A/C units
- 50A circuits: electric ranges, EV chargers

## Safety Notes
- **Never touch anything inside the panel with wet hands**
- **Do NOT remove the panel cover** — exposed bus bars carry lethal voltage even with main breaker off
- If you see **scorch marks, melted plastic, or smell burning** — leave the area and call an electrician immediately
- Keep the area around the panel clear — **3 feet clearance required by code**
- Breakers are rated for a maximum number of trips — repeatedly tripping shortens their life
