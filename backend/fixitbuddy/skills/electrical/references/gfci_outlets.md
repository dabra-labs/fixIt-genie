# GFCI Outlets

**Category:** Electrical
**Covers:** Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter outlets — bathroom, kitchen, outdoor, garage

## What Is a GFCI?

A GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) outlet monitors the difference in current between the hot and neutral wires. If it detects even a small imbalance (indicating current is taking an unintended path — possibly through a person), it trips in milliseconds to prevent electrocution.

*Camera cue: GFCI outlet has two buttons in the center — TEST (usually black) and RESET (usually red). Found in bathrooms, kitchens, garages, outdoor locations, and near water sources.*

## Diagnostic Steps

1. **Find the GFCI outlet** — it has TEST and RESET buttons in the center
   - *Camera cue: Usually in bathrooms, kitchens, garages, and outdoor locations*

2. **Press the RESET button firmly**
   - *Camera cue: You should hear a click and power should restore*

3. **If RESET won't click in** — the GFCI detected a fault
   - Unplug everything connected to that outlet first
   - Press TEST, then RESET
   - If it still won't reset, there's an active ground fault on the circuit

4. **Check upstream GFCI outlets** — one GFCI can protect multiple outlets downstream
   - *Camera cue: If bathroom outlets are dead, the protecting GFCI may be in another bathroom, garage, or at the panel*

5. **Test monthly:** Press TEST → outlet should go dead. Press RESET → power restores.

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Bathroom outlets all dead | GFCI tripped — could be in another bathroom or garage | Find and reset the protecting GFCI. Check ALL bathrooms, garage, and outdoor outlets |
| GFCI trips repeatedly | Persistent ground fault — faulty appliance or wiring | Unplug everything, reset. Plug in appliances one at a time to identify the culprit |
| GFCI trips when using hair dryer | Hair dryer pulling too much current or has a ground fault | Try a different hair dryer. If same issue, circuit may be overloaded or GFCI is overly sensitive |
| GFCI button won't stay in | Wiring issue or failed GFCI | Replace the GFCI outlet — they fail after ~10 years of tripping |
| Outdoor outlet dead | GFCI tripped — protecting outlet is usually inside | Find the indoor GFCI that protects outdoor circuits (often in garage or utility room) |

## GFCI Location Map (typical)
- Bathroom outlets → protected by GFCI in that bathroom or sometimes a shared bathroom
- Kitchen counter outlets → protected by GFCI outlet on same counter
- Garage outlets → protected by GFCI outlet in garage (often near entry door)
- Outdoor outlets → protected by GFCI inside (garage or utility room)
- Basement outlets → may be protected by GFCI in basement or garage

## Safety Notes
- **GFCI protects you from electrocution** — never bypass or remove it
- **Test GFCI outlets monthly** — they can fail silently (won't trip when needed)
- If a GFCI outlet is more than 10 years old and has been tripping frequently, replace it
- A GFCI that won't reset with nothing plugged in indicates a wiring fault — call an electrician
